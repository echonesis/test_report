Taiwan Technology: Semiconductors Trim estimates/TPs on weaker inventory replenishment demand in 2H23; Buy TSMC/UMC, Neutral on MediaTek

Anticipating a weaker demand recovery in 2H23 We are trimming our forecasts for TSMC, UMC and MediaTek mainly to reﬂect weaker than expected demand recovery in 2H23. While our key viewpoint remains unchanged that 1H23 will be the industry bottom followed by a demand recovery in 2H23, the momentum of demand resumption in 2H23 could be weaker than our earlier expectation, especially in PC and smartphone markets as our industry checks indicate customers are still conservative on inventory build.

Inventory correction cycle likely to last for longer For PC, although the stabilization of sell-through has translated to the reducing trend of inventory dollar for PC OEM/ODM since 2Q22 (see Exhibit 1), we see still lackluster inventory replenishment momentum from brand customers as they maintain a conservative stance on inventory control with a low conﬁdence level in inventory build. We believe this will also be impacted by ongoing macro uncertainties. As for smartphone, the industry dynamic looks similar to the PC market, according to our checks. Moreover, we did not see any meaningful new AI demand fulﬁlling utilization rate at TSMC as of now.

Overall speaking, we now believe the industry-wide inventory correction cycle is

sell-through, and for inventory to normalize for those companies with higher PC/smartphone exposure. See also: 4Q22 Inventory Tracker: OEM Inventory Down,

Cutting foundry UTR and capex forecasts Due to the weaker than expected demand outlook in 2H23, we have trimmed our assumptions for the capacity utilization rate (UTR) for both TSMC and UMC. We are now looking for 12-inch capacity UTR in 2H23 to be at 93-95% level (from 95-100%) for TSMC, and lower at 85-90% (from 88-97%) for UMC in 2H23. Furthermore, in light of slowing demand and UTR recovery, we see high possibility for a slowdown in capex spending for foundry names. Therefore, we cut our capex forecasts for both TSMC and UMC. TSMC (2330.TW)

Weaker near term momentum especially for 7nm We cut our 2023/2024/2025E estimates by 6.2%/4.6%/1.4% respectively, mainly as we reﬂect the weaker demand recovery in 2H23. We have lowered our 12-inch UTR for TSMC, and now look for UTR in 2H23 to be at the 93-95% level (from 95-100% previously). Speciﬁcally, we see a deeper cut at 7nm given a slower sell-in and

customers’ conservative stance towards inventory control. We now forecast UTR for 7nm to be ~55% in 2H23 (vs 65-75% previously, and 45-50% in 1H23), dragged by the lukewarm order momentum from smartphone.

On capex, in light of the weaker demand and slower UTR recovery, we trim TSMC’s 2023/2024/2025E capex to US$32bn/US$36bn/US$40bn (from US$33bn/US$42bn/US$44bn previously).

UMC (2303.TW)

Current expansion projects likely pushed out by 1-2 quarters We cut our 2022E/23E/24E earnings estimates by 11.0%/8.9%/8.6%, mainly to reﬂect a lower UTR for UMC in 2H23. We are now looking for its 12-inch capacity UTR in 2H23 to lower to 85-90% (from 88-97% previously and 80-82% in 1H23). The magnitude of UTR recovery in 2H23 for its 8-inch capacity will also lower to ~60% (vs 80-85% previously, and vs 55-58% in 1H23).

Furthermore, on capacity expansion, due to the slowing demand and UTR recovery, we believe it is very likely for UMC to push out its current expansion projects; for instance, for its P6 and Singapore expansion by 1-2 quarters. Accordingly, we lower its capex for 2023/2024E to US$2.4bn/US$2.2bn (from US$2.5bn/US$2.5bn previously).

Weaker 2023 outlook, but the rebound in 2024 should remain strong Post our downward earnings revisions, we are now looking for a 12.3% YoY revenue (in USD term) decline in 2H23, vs a 3.4% YoY decrease previously, and a 24.3% YoY drop in 1H23.
